Choice Piece

I've always loved how there has been a choice piece in portfolio presentations. For as long as I have been able to, I've tried to encorporate work outside of my school. I admit that part of this is that I think it's sneaky and feels like going behind the school's back, but I also believe there is some merit to it. My life doesn't end when I leave the school building; I'm entirely capable of learning off the school clock.

My choice piece this year is the video game I made (and am making) for the convention described in my Presentation and Design piece. The game is called DJ Clips' Ultimate Pipe Dream - here's a video.

The reason I put this as a choice peace is because I technically have been working on this since before the school year began. I showed off a much smaller game that I made last year, when the panelists were other students. This year's project really marks what I think is the beginning of my career in serious programming. What you see here was almost entirely made by me - bar some of the game's music. I started learning to code ruby around last June, and am really happy with where I am today. I would consider this project to be in "two parts", the other of which is covered in the "Design and Presentation" page. I should be bringing my laptop with me to the presentation in case you want to test out the game, or see any of the code I wrote.
