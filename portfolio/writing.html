Writing Piece - College WR 121 Essay

Reflection

Alright, so my presentation does have one school related item in it - but it isn't high school! My senior year class selection has been slightly limited - one of the reasons as to me choosing to focus on work outside of school. In order to fill up all the gaps in my schedule I began to take courses at PCC.

The essay you just read was the first college level essay I have written. The course I'm taking is called Writing 121 - English Compostion (An entirely ordinary name by my metric). The prompt for the piece was to assess and explain a piece of writing by Michael Kimmel on "The Guy Code", a smaller section of his book Guyland. My grading was a success - 96 out of 100 - but that's not to say I don't have any problems. Aside from some issues with the writing itself, which I will likely explain during the presentation, the structure of the essay asked us to write as if the author was correct regardless of our own thoughts on the matter. I understand that the assignment was mostly focused on our ability to write thesis statements and properly quote sources, but I don't agree with the philosophy of forcing students to believe any ideal in this way. As an introduction to college writing, I guess I could say I'm getting used to those "grin and bear it" sorts of assignments.
